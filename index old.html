What is cultured meat?

Cultured meat is laboratory grown meat. In the production process, animal stem cells and muscle cells are stimulated to multiply with special nutrients. Once there are enough muscle cells, they can be rearranged into structures that are similar to regular meat. It is biologically exactly the same as the meat tissue that comes from a cow.

Cultured meat looks just like regular meat.

The taste is convincingly similar to regular meat.

As cultured meat is also made from animal muscles, the texture is the same.

Facts

Benefits of cultured meat

Environmentally friendly

There is significantly less land used, and there is a significant decrease in greenhouse gas emissions and water usage compared to regular meat.

Animal friendly

Within the process animals’ cells are obtained from animals under anaesthesia such that no animals are harmed. Furthermore, cells from one single cow can produce the same amount of meat as 440.000 cows from traditional methods.

Healthier

It is made with natural ingredients and no unnatural chemicals are added. In addition, it doesn’t contain unhealthy remnants of vaccines that regular meat does have.
